<p><p>Superluminescent Emitting Diodes (SLEDs) are semiconductor light sources that emit light through spontaneous emission, offering broad spectral linewidths while maintaining a high degree of coherence. Unlike laser diodes, SLEDs combine the advantages of high brightness and low coherence, making them ideal for applications in optical coherence tomography (OCT), where precise imaging is essential.</p><p>The Superluminescent Emitting Diodes Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand in the healthcare sector, particularly in non-invasive medical imaging. Additionally, advancements in telecommunications, including fiber-optic systems, are fueling the need for efficient light sources that SLEDs provide. Overall, the growing adoption of SLEDs across industries highlights their versatility and the pivotal role they play in future optical technologies.</p></p>
