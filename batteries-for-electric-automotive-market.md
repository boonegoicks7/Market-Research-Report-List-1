<p><p>The electric automotive market primarily utilizes three battery types: Li-ion, NiMH, and lead-acid batteries. Li-ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density, long lifespan, and lightweight characteristics, making them ideal for modern electric vehicles. NiMH batteries, though heavier and less energy-dense, are commonly used in hybrid vehicles due to their reliability and cost-effectiveness. Lead-acid batteries, while inexpensive and widely available, are less efficient and heavier, mainly suited for low-speed electric vehicles and backup power applications.</p></p>
