<p><p>Preclinical Positron Emission Tomography (PET) encompasses two main market types: structural imaging and functional imaging. Structural imaging focuses on providing detailed anatomical views of tissues and organs, aiding in the assessment of physical abnormalities. In contrast, functional imaging examines biological processes by tracking metabolic activity, enabling researchers to observe how diseases progress and respond to treatments. Together, these imaging modalities support advancements in drug development and disease research in preclinical settings, enhancing understanding of various medical conditions.</p></p>
