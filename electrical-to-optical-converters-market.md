<p><p>Electrical to optical converters enable the conversion of electrical signals into optical signals for efficient data transmission. In the market, two main types are prevalent: multi-mode and single-mode converters. Multi-mode converters are designed for short-distance communication, utilizing wider core fibers to allow multiple light paths, making them cost-effective for local networks. In contrast, single-mode converters are optimized for long-distance transmission, using a narrower fiber core to support a single light path, resulting in higher bandwidth and reduced signal loss over greater distances.</p></p>
