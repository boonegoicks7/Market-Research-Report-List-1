<p><p>The Human DNA Vaccines market comprises two primary types: recombinant protein vaccines and gene-based vaccines. Recombinant protein vaccines involve the use of genetically engineered proteins derived from pathogens to stimulate an immune response. In contrast, gene-based vaccines introduce DNA sequences encoding specific antigens into the host's cells, prompting them to produce these antigens and trigger immunity. Both types leverage advanced biotechnology to enhance vaccine efficacy and safety, catering to diverse infectious diseases and therapeutic applications.</p></p>
