<p><p>Spin transport electronics, or spintronic devices, are categorized into metal-based and semiconductor-based devices. Metal-based devices leverage ferromagnetic materials to manipulate electron spins, offering advantages such as high speed and robustness. They are often used in memory applications like MRAM. In contrast, semiconductor-based devices utilize spin-polarized currents in semiconductors for functionalities in quantum computing and advanced transistors. This diversity in materials allows for tailored applications, enhancing performance and efficiency in various electronic components and systems across industries.</p></p>
