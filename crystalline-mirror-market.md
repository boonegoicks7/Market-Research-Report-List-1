<p><p>The crystalline mirror market primarily comprises two types of substrates: Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) and Indium Phosphide (InP). GaAs substrates are favored for their efficient light emission properties in optoelectronic applications, particularly in lasers and high-frequency devices. In contrast, InP substrates are preferred for high-speed and high-frequency applications, including telecommunications, due to their superior electron mobility. Both substrates play crucial roles in advancing technologies in photonics, telecommunications, and other electronic devices, driving market growth and innovation.</p></p>
